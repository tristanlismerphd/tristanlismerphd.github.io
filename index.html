// About

WHO
I
AM

Second-year PhD candidate at the Institute for Quantum Computing, working jointly with Kevin Resch and Rob Spekkens at Perimeter Institute.

My research lives at the intersection of quantum foundations and experimental photonics — building apparatus to ask nature its deepest questions.

Quantum Foundations Photonics GPT Theory Interferometry Born's Rule SPDC Sources

The Research

Experimentally testing higher-order interference across multi-slit configurations to probe Born's rule. Extracting Sorkin parameters using a GPT rank-fitting pipeline with ICCD camera data.

The Theory

Developing a framework for epistemic restrictions in Generalised Probabilistic Theories — asking what constraints on knowledge are sufficient to recover quantum-like structure.

The Lab

Ti:Sapphire at 790 nm, Sagnac SPDC sources, phase-encoded microscope-slide masks, PiMax 4 ICCD camera. Making quantum mechanics visible, one fringe pattern at a time.

The People

Advised by Kevin Resch (IQC) and Rob Spekkens (Perimeter Institute). Active in outreach — giving quantum optics talks to high school students across Ontario.

// Research & Work

WHAT I
BUILD

01
🔬

Multi-Slit Interference

Three and four-slit photonic experiments measuring the Sorkin parameter I₃ — testing whether quantum mechanics permits higher-order interference beyond Born's rule.

02
📐

GPT Rank Fitting

Truncated SVD pipeline fitting GPT state spaces to experimental data across 2–4 slit configurations. Identified rank-4 structure consistent with a qubit.

03
💡

Photonic Sources

Design and construction of Sagnac-loop SPDC entangled photon sources, interferometer alignment, and Ti:Sapphire laser operation at 790 nm.

04
∿

Epistemic Restrictions

Theoretical paper on the knowledge-balance principle on the gbit. Novel Trichotomy Theorem characterising which GPTs admit epistemic interpretations.

05
🎓

Outreach & Teaching

Quantum optics presentations to high school students across Ontario. Senior mentoring in the Resch lab. Coursework in quantum sensing and optomechanics.

06
⚙️

Python pipelines on Compute Canada clusters (Narval, Nibi) via SLURM for large-scale interference data processing and GPT state space fitting.
